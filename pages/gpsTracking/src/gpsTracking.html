Home GPS Card

The Home GPS Tracking Card shows bus owners the status of their buses at a glance. It displays information like bus number, whether it's on or off, the route status, and the distance traveled today, helping owners keep track easily.

  • Bus Number: Tells you which bus it is, like its name.
  • Ignition Status: Whether the bus is on or off.
  • Track Status: Shows if the bus is following its route or not.
  • Speed & Distance Today: Tells how fast the bus is going and how far it traveled today.
  • Location: Where the bus is right now, like its address.

FAQs

  • Why is the ignition status constantly shimmering on the Home GPS Card?
    The shimmer indicates that we are currently unable to retrieve the ignition status from the server. Once we receive the data, the shimmer will be replaced with the actual status.
  • I don't see any buses on the Home GPS Card. What could be the issue?
    This might be due to either no buses being linked to the logged-in user or the buses not having a device ID assigned. Please ensure your buses are correctly linked.
  • What does it mean when the Home GPS Card displays "Stopped since x mins" instead of the bus speed?
    This indicates that the bus is currently not moving. The timer shows the duration since the bus came to a halt.
  • The location address on the Home GPS Card seems outdated. How often is it updated?
    The address in this widget is only get updated when we came to this page, or we refresh the page using the refresh indicator.
  • Why do I see a "No Data Found" widget on the GPS Tracking List Page with no buses displayed?
    This might be due to either no buses being linked to the logged-in user or the buses not having a device ID assigned. Please ensure your buses are correctly linked.
  • I don't see any buses on the Home GPS Card. What could be the issue?
    The widget appears when no buses are linked to your account or if the buses lack a device ID. Ensure that your buses are correctly linked and have device IDs assigned
  • What does the "Play Journey" button do on the GPS bus card?
    Clicking the "Play Journey" button navigates you to the "History Replay Page," where you can visually review the historical route and details of a specific bus
  • How does the "Share Bus Live Location" feature work on the GPS Tracking List Page?
    This feature allows you to share the live location of a specific bus via a link, which remains valid for 6 hours. It's useful for sharing the real-time location with others.

  • Why are some buses continuously shimmering on the GPS Tracking List Page?
    Shimmering indicates ongoing loading or processing. If it persists, it might be due to change in the deviceId or temporary server issues. The shimmer will be replaced with the actual data once retrieved.
  • Can I see historical routes directly from the GPS Tracking List Page?
    Yes, by clicking on the GPS bus card, you'll be directed to the "History Replay Page," where you can access historical routes, real-time details, and trip statistics for a specific bus.
  • Why is the bus address not updating in real-time on the Live Tracking Page?
    The address is inherited from the previous page; it updates only when you revisit the page. All data refreshes at a 30-second interval.
  • Why does the map sometimes show a blurry or grey screen?
    This could be due to slow internet speed; a stable connection is needed for optimal map display.

  • Why is the Arrival/Departure time available only for certain towns on the Itinerary?
    The availability of these times depends on whether the API response includes these fields for specific towns.

  • Why is the bus on the map not moving sometimes?
    The bus movement relies on the Traccar live location trail. If the trail is not available, the bus will be unable to move on the map. In simple terms, we are not getting the updates from the Traccar.

  • Why does the "Is Trip ka Hisaab" button sometimes show an error toast?
    This occurs when there is no linked trip ID for the bus. Ensure a linked trip ID for accurate results.
